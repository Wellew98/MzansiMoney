5 Simple Ways to Save Money in South Africa

Saving money can be tough, but here are some practical tips for South Africans:

  • Shop smart: Buy in bulk at markets like Checkers or Shoprite for staples like rice and mealie meal.
  • Cut transport costs: Use public transport or carpool instead of driving everywhere.
  • Save on electricity: Switch to LED bulbs and use a geyser timer to reduce your Eskom bill.
  • Start a stokvel: Join a community savings group to pool money for big expenses.
  • Grow your own food: Plant veggies like spinach or tomatoes in your backyard to save on groceries.

Small changes can add up to big savings over time!

Understanding VAT in South Africa

VAT, or Value Added Tax, is a 15% tax added to most goods and services in South Africa. Here’s what you need to know:

  • It’s charged on everything from groceries to clothing, but essentials like bread and milk are often exempt.
  • Businesses with an annual turnover above R1 million must register for VAT with SARS.
  • You can claim VAT back on business expenses if you’re registered—just keep your receipts!
